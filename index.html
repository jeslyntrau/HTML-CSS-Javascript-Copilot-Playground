Fresh Fruit Sponge Cake Recipe

Perfect for any summer gathering!




This light, airy sponge cake is perfect for any occasion, filled with whipped cream and your favorite fresh fruits.


Ah, summer nights at Grandma’s backyard—those memories are forever etched in my heart, like the smell of warm grass and the sound of cicadas humming in the distance. As the sun dipped behind the hills, we'd light up the bonfire and wait for the magic of the evening to unfold. There was always something special about those nights—s'mores crackling over the flames, the scent of BBQ in the air, and, of course, her famous whipped cream and fresh fruit cake. Oh, that cake!

Grandma’s cake wasn’t just a dessert; it was the sweet punctuation mark to every summer adventure. We’d spend the day running barefoot in the grass, collecting wildflowers, and building forts under the big oak tree. By dusk, our cheeks were sticky with popsicle residue, and fireflies began their twilight dance around us. But no matter how late it got, when Grandma carried that cake out to the picnic table, it felt like the real fun was just beginning. I remember the way the layers of sponge cake were always so perfectly light, like they could float right off the plate. Grandma had this way of folding the batter just so, making the cake impossibly fluffy but never dry. And that whipped cream—oh my! It was never too sweet, always just enough to leave you dreaming of clouds and summer kisses. She’d crown it with berries so fresh they still tasted of sunshine—strawberries, raspberries, and slices of kiwi fanned out like tiny emeralds glinting in the moonlight.

After the bonfire had burned down to embers, we’d sit around the patio with plates in our laps, digging into that cake by candlelight. I swear there was a kind of magic in every bite—the soft sponge, the cool whipped cream, the burst of juicy berries. It wasn’t just a dessert; it was happiness on a plate. And somehow, no matter how full we were from ribs and grilled corn, there was always room for a second slice (or a third... but who’s counting?). Sometimes, Grandma would let us "help" with the cake earlier in the day. That usually meant sneaking spoonfuls of whipped cream when her back was turned or eating half the fruit before it ever made it to the cake. She’d laugh and tell us, "A little taste test never hurt anyone." And she'd ruffle our hair with that sparkle in her eye that always made me feel like I was exactly where I was supposed to be



Ingredients

For the sponge cake:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 4 large eggs (room temperature)
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup whole milk (warmed)
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter (melted and cooled slightly)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

For the whipped cream:

  • 2 cups heavy cream (cold)
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

For the fruit filling and decoration:

  • 2 cups fresh fruit (sliced)
  • Extra sliced fruit for garnish on top (optional)

Instructions

Step One: Prepare the Sponge Cake

  • Preheat the oven to 350°F and grease two 8-inch cake pans, lining the bottoms with parchment paper.

Step Two: Mix Dry Ingredients

  • In a bowl, sift together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Set bowl of dry ingredients aside.

Step Three: Whip the Eggs and Sugar

  • In a large bowl, beat the eggs and sugar using a mixer on high speed until the mixture is pale, thick, and triples in volume (about 8-10 minutes).

Step Four: Add Wet Ingredients

  • Gradually fold in the flour mixture using a spatula, being careful not to deflate the batter. In a separate bowl, combine the warm milk, melted butter, and vanilla extract.

Step Five: Bake the Cake

  • Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans.
  • Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

Step Six: Cool the Cake

  • Allow the cakes to cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

Step Seven: Prepare the Whipped Cream

  • In a chilled bowl, beat the heavy cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract until stiff peaks form. Be careful not to overbeat.

Step Eight: Prepare the Fruit

  • Wash and slice the fruit you'll use for filling and/or decoration.

Step Nine: Assemble the Cake

  • Place the first layer of cake on a serving plate and spread a generous amount of whipped cream on top, followed by sliced fruit pieces.
  • Place the second layer of cake on top.

Step Ten: Decorate the Top

  • Use any extra fruit and whipped cream to create a decorative topping.

Serve and Enjoy! This recipe is highly customizable: you can use any combination of fruits you love! Feel free to experiment with other elements, such as adding a drizzle of fruit syrup or lemon curd between the layers.
